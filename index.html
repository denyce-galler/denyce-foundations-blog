Get to know me

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I had a recent instance where I worked with a colleague that had made decisions that affected the nursing care provided. I had come to realise this as I worked a lot more closely with them and it had come to the point where it was evident that it was impacting not only patient care but also the ability of the nursing team to work together. I then made the decision to bring this issue up with the team leader. Looking back I feel glad that I was virtuous in this situation as I knew that it was the right thing to do and I hope that I continue to make decisions like this when faced with the same problem.

  • Love
  • Empathy

I accredit my friends and family in helping me live these values out. They help me remind myself of how important and valuable these values can be.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I would like to think I have a lot of courage and optimism when it comes to challenges. Regardless of where I am at with a task or if I am faced with a problem, I always believe that I can get through it and that I will be fine no matter what happens. I think this kind of mindset will greatly assist with my learning journey especially since I am at ground zero with my knowledge in coding.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

In terms of my learning, a limitation that I have is that I lose focus if content is not interesting/something I am passionate about. I can see how this will impede my ability to keep my attention in something that is important. Same goes with my career development. If I lose passion or there aren’t areas that are interesting to me, then it can affect how I feel about moving forward in the industry.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

Nursing has given me a lot of opportunities to work with an array of patients. Therefore, i’ve experienced many instances where I am met with resistance especially from patients who refuse to work with me when carrying out their care. I find my reactions tend to be somewhat of a peace-keeper so I lean towards more of de-escalation approaches such as acting level-headed. I find that this type of approach can be quite effective especially if emotions are heightened.
