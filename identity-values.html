Identity and Values

  • Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

Back when I was doing Nursing, I had a colleague that had made decisions that affected the Nursing care provided. I had come to realise this as I worked a lot more closely with them and it had come to the point where I realised it had to be addressed as it impacting not only patient care but also the ability of the team to work together. I then brought this issue up with the team leader. Looking back I am glad that I chose to be virtuous in this situation

  • Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

Values that are important to me are love and empathy. I accredit my friends and family for helping me live and carry out these values because they are constant reminders as to how powerful and valuable they are.

  • Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

I would like to think I have a lot of courage and optimism when it comes to challenges. Regardless of where I am at something or what problem I am facing, I always have belief within myself that I can get through it and that I will be fine no matter what happens. I think this kind of mindset will greatly assist with my learning journey especially since I have very little knowledge about coding.

  • Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

In terms of my learning, a limitation that I have is that I lose focus if content is not interesting/something I am passionate about. I can see how this will impede my ability to hold my attention on something that is important. Same goes with my career development. If I lose passion or there aren’t areas that are interesting to me, then it can affect how I feel about moving forward in the industry.

  • Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

Nursing has given me a lot of opportunities to work with an array of patients. Therefore, i’ve experienced many instances where I am met with resistance especially from patients who refuse to work with me when carrying out their care. I find my reactions tend to be somewhat of a peace-keeper so I lean towards more of de-escalation approaches such as acting emotions displayed are intense.
